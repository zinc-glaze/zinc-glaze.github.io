About Me

Front End Web Developer with a background in library archives, music, and audio. Effective at combining creativity and problem-solving to develop user-friendly applications. Known for my passion for technology and design, intuitive aesthetic sense, determined and methodical approach to troubleshooting, and keen attention to detail.


I recently graduated from the UNC-Chapel Hill Coding Bootcamp, an intensive 24-week course in full-stack web development. My current focus is on working with React in conjunction with various frameworks (Semantic UI, Materialize, Bootstrap), as well as custom CSS, to create clean, intuitive, and functional user interfaces. My training in Node.js, Express.js, MySQL, and MongoDB allows me to integrate these interfaces into a full-stack MERN application adhering to REST API standards.


My goal is to find a job developing web applications for arts, cultural heritage, and educational institutions and projects. My combination of technical experience and facility (working on the Digital Collections and Repository teams at Duke Libraries), collaborative approach (honed over many years as a performing musician), and ability to learn quickly will position me to be successful in this role.
